## Adopted DSET-native principles

1. A promoted proof supports one named claim for one intended use and records
   work/producer, method/setup, context, observation window, exact identity,
   evidence polarity, currentness, and reopen trigger.
2. Counter-evidence or changed inputs stale only the affected claim closure;
   unsupported conclusions remain pending or uncertain.
3. A Decision retains rationale while normative consequences return to their
   owning Requirements, Contracts, Design, proof plans, or operating rules.
   Reopen and supersession preserve prior evidence and use explicit successor
   links instead of silently editing history.
4. Candidate comparison preserves incomparable, inconclusive, and
   evidence-needed states. Adopt/adapt/reject belongs to the Decision, not to an
   artificially forced comparison winner.
5. Risk scales review depth without deleting baseline gates. Generated views
   remain non-authoritative and state captured/omitted structure, permitted and
   prohibited use, canonical return path, and currentness when relied upon.
6. Reversible ordinary work may return a bounded result without a committed
   Change; reliance-bearing work requires durable artifacts.
